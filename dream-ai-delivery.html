How I worked with AI

The criteria were mine. The output was the AI's. The decisions were mine.

I used AI as a series of specialist collaborators — each with a defined role, a specific criteria set, and a clear handoff. Research before tools. Design before code. Evaluation before advancing.

Research before tools.

IRT protocol, the veteran audience's relationship with help-seeking, and the failure modes of mental health apps — studied before any tool was opened. That research became the criteria every AI output was measured against. Mobbin for mobile UX patterns. 21st.dev for component reference.

Design before code.

Claude Design locked the visual system before Claude Code opened. Colors, typography, motion rules, component specs — handed over as a non-negotiable foundation. No values invented during build.

Evaluation before advancing.

Every output — from Claude Design and Claude Code — was evaluated by a separate Claude instance before advancing. Not a technical review: does this serve a combat veteran or a survivor of assault at 2am? Failures named by specific element. No screen advanced until it passed. That's what the amber line in the timeline represents.

The process

Step 01

Research and clinical grounding

IRT protocol. Nightmare disorder as an independent suicidality risk factor in veterans. How mental health apps fail this audience before earning trust. Every design decision has a clinical reason — this is where those reasons were established.

Step 02

Prompt chain — before any tool was opened

14 prompts — context, constraints, orchestration, skeleton, every screen, two-persona critical review — written and evaluated before any tool was opened. The build began only when the chain was complete.

Step 03

Visual direction — Claude Design

Three expressions of Vigil proposed. I chose The Threshold — darkness with a ground beneath it. After a nightmare, having a surface to stand on is the exit signal. Not an aesthetic choice; a clinical one.

Foundation — Claude Code

Design system as CSS tokens. Frame, navigation, persistent elements, microphone pulse — all before any screen. The pulse deceleration is a clinical specification: gradual deceleration supports nervous system regulation.

Step 06

The prototype

Record → Analyze → Deepen → Rescript → Watch → Heal. Watch treated as the hero — the moment the clinical mechanism is felt, not explained. Ten nights. One word, entirely the user's.

Prototype walkthrough — part 2 ~5 min

Three decisions worth naming

01

No onboarding. The Record screen is the onboarding.

For an audience that resists help-seeking, explanation before action is weight they weren't carrying. The screen's job is to already be ready. The microphone is there. Nothing else.

02

Vigil with inhabited warmth — not cold, not soft.

Coldness reads as abandonment. Performed warmth reads as condescension. The direction: warmth that was already here before the user arrived. Residual, not emanating. The veteran reads it as vigilance. The survivor reads it as a space that was already safe.

03

The word comes from inside the nightmare, not after it.

In Deepen Q2, the user names what they feel at the worst moment. That word — typed, never spoken — flows verbatim into Watch and the Summary. Captured inside the nightmare, before any rescript. It cannot be faked or generated. It can only come from the user, at that moment.

04

Night 10 shows the distance between two answers.

What the user called the feeling on Night 1. What they say is different on Night 10. The summary is the space between those two answers — not a celebration, not a score. After "Carry it.", everything fades except their word. Then it goes. "Come back when you need to." The product doesn't close. The user does.

The prompt chain

Context and foundation — Claude Code
P1 · Dream AI Context
Anchors every session in the user, the clinical mechanism, and Vigil as a decision filter — not an aesthetic. Pasted once at the start of every Claude Code session.
P2 · Dream AI Constraints
Active reference pasted before every screen. Mobile frame, motion rules, copy rule, persistent elements, evaluation criteria. The engineer consults this per screen, not once.
P3 · Orchestrator
Forces the AI to name each screen's emotional job and build tier before writing a line of code. A build that begins on a misunderstanding produces screens that can't talk to each other.
P4 · Skeleton
CSS tokens, frame, navigation, persistent elements, microphone pulse — before any screen. Everything inherits from this.
Screen prompts — Claude Code
P5 · Record · Tier 1
First act of trust. Three states, two user entry modes, one clinical pulse. The screen's job is to already be ready.
P6 · Analyze · Tier 2
The product's first response. One flowing synthesis in the sleep pattern register — never therapy, never assessment, never a verdict.
P7 · Deepen · Tier 3
Two rounds of questions, each independently skippable. Four questions that open something without demanding anything.
P8 · Rescript · Tier 1
The pivot. Three exits from the nightmare, one suggested by position only. The word that goes into the film and the Summary was captured in Deepen Q2 — when the user named the feeling at the worst moment. Rescript is the choice of direction, not the capture of the word.
P9 · Watch · Tier 1
The hero. Zero UI during playback. Particles, ambient sound, narration in Ground sequence. The user's word woven into arrival — verbatim, never announced.
P10 · Heal + Transformation Summary
Night 1: mechanism and reminder. Nights 2–9: ritual, nothing added. Night 10: one question, then the distance between two answers.
Review — Claude Code
P11 · Critical Review
The AI stops being the engineer. Two walkthroughs — combat veteran, then survivor of assault. Findings named by specific element. No fixes until findings are confirmed and approved.
Visual direction — Claude Design
CD-1 · Emotional Direction
Three expressions of Vigil proposed before any color or type decision. One chosen and locked as the anchor for everything that follows.
CD-2 · Design System Foundation
Every token justified against the user at 2am. Color, typography, spacing, motion — derived from the emotional direction. No value invented during build.
CD-3 · Components and Screen References
Five components at every state. Three screen references named by what they remove, not what they contain.

From the Critical Review prompt

"'The copy feels off' is not a finding. 'The word processing in the Analyze transition implies the app is evaluating the recording' is a finding."

Prototype limitation — noted consciously

The suggested ending is mocked

Hardcoded as "The Feeling Backs Down" for all users in this prototype. In production, determined by AI analysis of the recording and Deepen responses — matching the emotional hotspot to the ending most likely to resolve it.

UX — what the prototype defers

The experience beyond the first 10 nights

Reminder editing after Night 1. Settings layer — delete, progress, IRT information. What happens after Night 10. Pause state for missed nights. VoiceOver and TalkBack support. Haptic feedback on the ritual entry and "Carry it." — not achievable in HTML.

Product — decisions that need owners

The questions this prototype surfaces but doesn't answer

Can the user begin again after Night 10? How does the product handle relapse? Multiple active nightmares? Clinician summaries? Each requires a therapist and a product team in the same room — not a prototype.

Clinical — what a real product requires

The work that happens before launch

IRT psychologist validation. Efficacy study with veterans and assault survivors. VA prescription integration. Crisis protocol for acute risk. HIPAA-compliant storage. A real AI-generated film — which this prototype mocks with particles and narration.
